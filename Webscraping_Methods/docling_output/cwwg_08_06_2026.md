## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  04  June  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 54.726 billion cubic meter, which is 29.81% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 93.86 percent of last  year's  storage  and  118.58  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is less than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole but is better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
- b) Groundwater  Levels :  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's  monitoring  stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.

## Key Insights

The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.

About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.

The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, and Nagaland.
