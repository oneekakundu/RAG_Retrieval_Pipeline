## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  2  July  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 47.725 billion cubic meters, which is 26.00% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 71.20 percent of last  year's  storage  and  98.60  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is less than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole but is better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
- b) Groundwater Levels: The groundwater situation is largely stable across the country. Out of 9,085 monitoring wells,  87.8%  (7,977  wells)  fall  under  the  normal  category,  while  12.2%  of  the  wells exhibit varying levels of groundwater drought.

Approximately  69%  of  the  wells  have  groundwater  levels  within  10  metres  below  ground  level, whereas  only  about  4%  have  groundwater  levels  deeper  than  40  metres, indicating  good groundwater availability across most regions.

Groundwater  drought  is  predominantly  mild  and  confined  to  limited  areas.  Among  the  affected wells,  7.2%  are  classified  as  experiencing  mild  drought,  2.2%  moderate  drought,  0.9%  severe drought, and 1.9% extreme drought.

## Key Insights

According to the Pre-Monsoon 2026 Groundwater Drought Index, groundwater conditions across the country are predominantly normal, with groundwater stress restricted to limited areas in a few states.
