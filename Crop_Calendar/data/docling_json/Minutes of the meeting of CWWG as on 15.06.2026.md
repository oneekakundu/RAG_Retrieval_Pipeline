## 1. Kharif Season Sowing Status:

-  Kharif  sowing  data  collection  has  started  from  05 th June,  2026  and  the  sowing  is  in  preliminary stage. ## 2. Weather Update:

- a) Temperature: Maximum temperature is likely to be above normal in Madhya Maharashtra, East India, Rajasthan and many parts of Northeast India.
- b) Rainfall Distribution: From June 01- 14 June 2026, the All-India cumulative rainfall is 8% lower than  its  Long  Period  Average  (LPA),  with  regional  variations:  (i)  East  &amp;  Northeast  India: (-) 40%; (ii) Northwest India: 13%; (iii) Central India: (-) 55%; and South Peninsula: (-) 4%.
3.  The seasonal trough at mean sea level from  West Rajasthan to Gangetic West Bengal across northeast Rajasthan, north Madhya Pradesh, south Uttar Pradesh, Bihar and Jharkhand persists.
4.  Isolated  heavy  to  very  heavy  rainfall  likely  over  Sub-Himalayan  West  Bengal  &amp;  Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh and Assam &amp; Meghalaya during next 6-7 days.
5.  Rainfall activity is likely in South India, some parts of Maharashtra, NE India, some parts of Central India, J&amp;K,  and isolated places of Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, and Uttarakhand.
6.  Rainfall  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  in  Kerala,  some  parts  of  South  Interior  Karnataka, Arunachal  Pradesh,  while  it  is  below  normal  in  coastal  Karnataka,  Konkan-Goa,  Gujarat, Odisha and East &amp; Northeast India.
7.  Isolated  to  Scattered  rainfall  likely  over  many  parts  of  Northwest  India  and  some  parts  of Central India during 14 th - 20 th June.
8.  Heat wave conditions very likely in isolated  pockets  over Marathawada and  Vidarbha during next 3-4 days.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  11  June  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 51.917 billion cubic meter, which is 28.28% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 91.83 percent of last  year's  storage  and  115.80  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is less than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole but is better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
- b) Groundwater  Levels :  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's  monitoring  stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20  m  are observed in around 13.5% of stations  of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.

## Key Insights

The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.

About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.

The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, and Nagaland.
