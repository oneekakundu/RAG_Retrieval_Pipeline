## Weekly report/ Minutes of the Crop Weather Watch Group (CWWG) meeting as on 06.04.2026

## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on April 03, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 58.29 lakh ha as compared to 57.80 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 30.12 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
-  As on 27.03.2026, 32.26% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. Approximately 67% area  of  Pulses  mainly  in  Gram  (66%),  Urdbean  (86%)  &amp;  Moong  (63%);  71.56%  area  of  Oilseeds majorly in Rapeseed &amp; Mustard (75.53%); 38.31% area of Shri Anna cum Coarse Cereals majorly in Jowar (54.47%), 25.57% area of Rice and 6.00% area of Wheat  has been harvested for Rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

Temperature: Maximum Temperature is likely to be normal to above-normal maximum temperatures (1.6-3⁰C) are likely over parts of Ladakh, along the west coast (Konkan &amp; Goa &amp; isolated places in coastal Karnataka), Coastal Odisha &amp; adjoining north Coastal Andhra Pradesh &amp; south Gangetic West Bengal,  some  parts  of  coastal  Tamil  Nadu,  some  parts  of  north  Bihar  &amp;  adjoining  northeast  Uttar Pradesh  and  many  parts  of  northeast  India  (Assam,  Meghalaya,  Nagaland,  Manipur,  Mizoram  &amp; Tripura).  Below  normal  to  appreciably  below  normal  maximum  temperatures  are  likely  over  central India and adjoining north peninsular India. Normal to below-normal weekly maximum temperatures are likely across the rest of the country.

- a) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  April  05  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  20% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 52%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 9%; (iii) Central India: 33%; and South Peninsula: 27%.
2.  A fresh Western Disturbance is likely to affect Northwest India from 07th April, 2026.
3.  Wet spell activity with thunderstorm &amp; lightning very likely over central, east &amp; peninsular India till 09th April with isolated hailstorm over Chhattisgarh on 05th; West Madhya Pradesh on 08th; Sub Himalayan West Bengal &amp; Sikkim on 05th, 07th &amp; 08th; Bihar on 06th &amp; 08th; Jharkhand during 06th-08th April, 2026. Isolated heavy rainfall likely over Sub-Himalayan West Bengal &amp; Sikkim on 08th &amp; 09th April.
4.  Day temperatures are likely to remain below normal to near normal over most part of the country during next 7 days.
5.  Widespread  rainfall  activity  likely  over  Northeast  India,  Jammu  &amp;  Kashmir  adjoining  Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Tamil Nadu and Isolated rainfall activity over Uttarakhand &amp; adjoining plains of Western Himalayan region, Central India, Odisha &amp; adjoining Coastal Andhra Pradesh.
6.  Rainfall, Thunderstorm &amp; Hailstorm activity over Northwest India, Central, &amp; East India. Isolated heavy rainfall over Northeast India.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status:  As  on  02  April  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 85.698 billion cubic meter, which is 46.69% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 116.44 percent of

last  year's  storage  and  126.67  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.

-  Groundwater  Levels:  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
-  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
-  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
-  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests &amp; Diseases : The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level.
