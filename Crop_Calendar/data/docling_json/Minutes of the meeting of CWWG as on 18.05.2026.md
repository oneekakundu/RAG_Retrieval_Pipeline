## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on May 15, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 83.08 lakh ha as compared to 80.01 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 31.05 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year. There is also an increase in the area sown under Maize by about 1.50 lakh ha so far from the last 5-years average.
-  As on 15.05.2026, 96.86% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. Pulses harvest has been completed; 99.87% area of Oilseeds- majorly in Rapeseed &amp; Mustard (100%); 98.62% area of Shri Anna cum Coarse Cereals- majorly in Jowar (99.66%), 72.70% area of Rice and 98.80% area of Wheat has been harvested for Rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

- a) Temperature:  Maximum  temperature  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  over  North  West  India, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, northern part of Coastal Andhra Pradesh, East India and many parts of North East India.
- b) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  May  17  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  4% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 10%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 4%; (iii) Central India: 30%; and South Peninsula: (-) 12%.
3.  Isolated  heavy  to  very  heavy  rainfall  also  likely  over  northeast  India  and  over  Andaman  &amp; Nicobar Islands, Sub-Himalayan West Bengal &amp; Sikkim during next 6-7 days; and over Tamil Nadu,  Puducherry  &amp;  Karaikal,  Kerala  &amp;  Mahe,  Lakshadweep  and  South  Interior  Karnataka during next 3 days.
4.  Scattered  to  Isolated  light  to  moderate  rainfall  with  thunderstorm,  lightning  &amp;  gusty  winds likely over Jammu-Kashmir on 19th &amp; 20th; Uttarakhand during 20th -23rd May.
5.  Isolated  light  to  moderate  rainfall  with  thunderstorm,  lightning  &amp;  gusty  winds  likely  over Chhattisgarh  and  Madhya Maharashtra during 18 th May  and  Himachal  Pradesh  during 19th  23rd May.
6.  Heat wave to severe heat wave conditions likely to prevail over plains of northwest India and central India during many days of the week.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  14  May  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 63.232 billion cubic meter, which is 34.45% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 112.91 percent of last  year's  storage  and  123.86  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
2.  Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
3.  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
4.  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
5.  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests  &amp;  Diseases :  Intensity  of  pests  and  diseases  at  some  fields  has  been  reported  to  be  above Economic Threshold Level for Litchi (Stinkbug and Mealy bug) in Bihar and for Rice (Neck Blast) in Assam. The Advisory has been issued by the survey team to the farms and regular monitoring is being carried out. The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level
