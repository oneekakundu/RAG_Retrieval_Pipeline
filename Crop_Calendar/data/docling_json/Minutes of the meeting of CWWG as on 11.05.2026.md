## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on May 08, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 83.08 lakh ha as compared to 80.01 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 31.05 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
-  As on 08.05.2026, 96.36% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. 100% area of Pulses has been completed; 99.87% area of Oilseeds majorly in Rapeseed &amp; Mustard (100%); 98.02% area of Shri Anna cum Coarse Cereals majorly in Jowar (99.66%), 65.70% area of Rice and 98.00% area of Wheat has been harvested for rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

- a) Temperature:  Maximum  temperature  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  in many  parts  of  Northwest India, Gujarat state, Konkan-Goa, coastal-Karnataka and Northeast India.
- b) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  May  10  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  6% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 12%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 3%; (iii) Central India: 41%; and South Peninsula: (-) 9%.
3.  A low-pressure area is likely to form over southwest Bay of Bengal during the next 48 hours.
4.  Isolated thunderstorms, lightning &amp; gusty winds likely over northwest India during 10th -14th May, 2026. Isolated hailstorm activity also likely over Jammu &amp; Kashmir, Ladakh on 11th &amp; 12th; Himachal Pradesh &amp; Uttarakhand on 12th &amp; 13th May.
5.  Isolated heavy rainfall likely over Arunachal Pradesh and Assam &amp; Meghalaya, Tamil Nadu, Puducherry &amp; Karaikal and Kerala &amp; Mahe.
6.  Heat wave conditions at isolated places likely over Jammu &amp; Kashmir on 10th; West Rajasthan, Gujarat during 10th -14th; East Rajasthan during 10th -13th; West Madhya Pradesh during 12th -14th May, 2026.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  07  May  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 66.830 billion cubic meter, which is 36.41% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 114.10 percent of last  year's  storage  and  124.84  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
2.  Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
3.  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
4.  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
5.  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests  &amp;  Diseases :  Intensity  of  pests  and  diseases  at  some  fields  has  been  reported  to  be  above Economic  Threshold  Level  for  Litchi  (Stinkbug  and  Mealy  bug)  in  Bihar.  The  Advisory  has  been issued by the survey team to the farms and regular monitoring is being carried out. The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level
