## 1. Kharif Season Sowing Status:

## 2. Weather Update:

- a) Temperature: Maximum temperature is likely to be above normal in Chhattisgarh, Odisha, East &amp; West UP, Haryana-Chandigarh-Delhi, Himachal Pradesh, East MP, Saurashtra &amp; Kutch and Vidarbha.
- b) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  June  01-  24  June  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  42% lower than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: (-) 41%; (ii) Northwest India: -22%; (iii) Central India: -59%; and South Peninsula: -28%.
3.  Very heavy to extremely Heavy rainfall likely over northeast India and over Sub-Himalayan till 30th June and Goa, Konkan, Madhya Maharashtra &amp; Vidarbha, Karnataka and MP till 5 July 2026.
4.  Moderate to severe thunderstorm activity accompanied by gusty winds, with moderate to severe Lightning activity is likely over North India, Jharkhand, Odisha, Marathwada and some parts of North-west India.
5.  Heat wave conditions likely over Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Haryana, Chandigarh and Delhi till 30 th June 2026.
6.  The seasonal trough at mean sea level from Punjab to Bihar now runs Punjab to Gangetic West Bengal across Haryana, Uttar Pradesh and Bihar.
7.  Overall, rainfall activity is likely to remain below normal over most parts of the country. A fresh Western Disturbance is likely to affect Northwest India from 2 nd July 2026.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  25  June  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 48.405 billion cubic meter, which is 26.37% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 73.21 percent of last  year's  storage  and  105.67  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is less than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole but is better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
- b) Groundwater Levels: In January, 2026, around 75.8% of the country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are  observed  in  around  13.5%  of  stations  of  the  country  covering  mainly  the  western  states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.

The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.

## Key Insights

About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.

The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, and Nagaland.
