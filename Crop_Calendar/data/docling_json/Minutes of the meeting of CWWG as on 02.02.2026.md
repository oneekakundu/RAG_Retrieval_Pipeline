Deeper  water  levels  of  more  than  20  m  are  observed  in  around  5%  of  stations  of  the country  covering  mainly  the  western  states,  especially  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
3.  The deeper groundwater level in the states like Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh  during  Post-monsoon  2025  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over extraction of groundwater compared to the recharge during the monsoon period.
4.  Annual  water  level  comparison  with  the  previous  year  Post-monsoon  2024  to  Postmonsoon  2025  has  shown  that  about  63%  of  total  analyzed  stations  of  the  country experienced rise in  annual  water level fluctuation may  be  attributed to the less groundwater extraction compared to 2024.
5.  About 73% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level  in  the  decadal  mean  water  level  fluctuation  of  2015-2024  with  respect  to  Postmonsoon 2025.
6.  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in Post-monsoon  2025 ,  groundwater conditions  across  India  remained  largely  stable,  with 91%  of  monitoring  stations showing no  deficit .
