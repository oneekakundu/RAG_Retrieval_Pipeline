## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on April 17, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 69.06 lakh ha as compared to 66.14 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 30.64 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
-  As on 17.04.2026, 81.69% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. Approximately 97% area  of  Pulses  mainly  in  Gram  (98%),  Urdbean  (98%)  &amp;  Moong  (82%);  98.41%  area  of  Oilseeds majorly  in  Rapeseed  &amp; Mustard  (100%); 81.58%  area of  Shri  Anna cum  Coarse Cereals  majorly in Jowar  (91.44%),  47.89%  area  of  Rice  and  75.00%  area  of  Wheat  has  been  harvested  for  rabi  crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

Temperature:  Maximum  temperature  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  in  Northwest  India, East  India, Chhattisgarh, Konkan-Goa, coastal Karnataka, many parts of Kerala, Northern parts of coastal Andhra Pradesh and some parts of Tamil Nadu.

- a) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  April  19  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  6% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 19%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 5%; (iii) Central India: 25%; and South Peninsula: (-) 16%.
2.  A fresh Western Disturbance is likely to affect Western Himalayan region from 23 rd April.
3.  Isolated  heavy  to  very  heavy  rainfall  likely  over  Arunachal  Pradesh  and  Assam  on  20 th and 21 st April.
4.  Scattered to Fairly widespread light to moderate rainfall/snowfall with thunderstorm, lightning &amp; gusty winds likely over Jammu-Kashmir-Ladakh-Gilgit-Baltistan-Muzaffarabad during 23rd-25th and Isolated to Scattered over Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand during the period.
5.  Thunderstorms activity likely to continue during the week  along east coast of India, as well as coastal areas of Kerala and Karnataka.
6.  Widespread rainfall activity likely over Northeast India, Western Himalayan Region and Isolated rainfall activity over Gujarat and Western parts of Peninsular India..
7.  Heat  wave  conditions  very  likely  in  isolated  pockets  of  West  Madhya  Pradesh  &amp;  Vidarbha, Chhattisgarh on 19th &amp; 20th; East Uttar Pradesh, East Rajasthan, East  Madhya Pradesh during 19th-23rd;  Jharkhand  on  20th  &amp;  21st;  West  Uttar  Pradesh  during  20th-22nd;  West  Rajasthan during 20th-23rd and Punjab, Haryana, Chandigarh, Odisha during 21st-23rd April.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  16  April  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 78.481 billion cubic meter, which is 42.75% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 116.25 percent of last  year's  storage  and  127.20  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last

year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.

-  Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
-  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
-  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
-  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests &amp; Diseases : The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level.
