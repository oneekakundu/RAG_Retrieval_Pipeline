## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on April 10, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 64.10 lakh ha as compared to 63.33 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 30.59 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
-  As on 27.03.2026, 32.26% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. Approximately 67% area  of  Pulses  mainly  in  Gram  (66%),  Urdbean  (86%)  &amp;  Moong  (63%);  71.56%  area  of  Oilseeds majorly in Rapeseed &amp; Mustard (75.53%); 38.31% area of Shri Anna cum Coarse Cereals majorly in Jowar (54.47%), 25.57% area of Rice and 6.00% area of Wheat  has been harvested for Rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

Temperature: Maximum temperature is likely to be above normal in Tamil Nadu, many parts of Andhra Pradesh, many parts of Odisha, Chhattisgarh and coastal regions of Karnataka.

- a) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  April  12  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  22% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 40%; (ii) Northwest India: 3%; (iii) Central India: 51%; and South Peninsula: 11%.
2. o A fresh  Western  Disturbance  is  likely  to  affect  Western  Himalayan  Region  from  15 th April, 2026.
3. o Scattered rainfall with thunderstorms, lightning, and gusty winds is expected across many parts of  Northwest,  Northeast,  East,  and  South  Peninsular  India  between  9-13  April,  with  stronger winds reaching 50-60 kmph in some eastern regions.
4. o Heavy rainfall is likely at isolated places over Arunachal Pradesh, South Assam, Tripura, and Sikkim, while hailstorm activity may occur over Assam &amp; Meghalaya on 9 April.
5. o Light  to  moderate  rain  with  thunderstorms  will  also  affect  parts  of  Tamil  Nadu,  Kerala, Rayalaseema, and South Interior Karnataka during this period.
6. o Gradual rise in maximum temperatures likely over Northwest, Central &amp; East India by 3-6°C during next 4-5 days. As a result, maximum temperatures are likely to be above normal over these region after 2 days.
7. o Heat wave conditions very likely in isolated pockets of Saurashtra  &amp; Kutch on 13th; Odisha during 14th -16th; Madhya Pradesh, Vidarbha, Chhattisgarh during 16th -18th; Marathawada on 16th April.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status:  As  on  10  April  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 82.070 billion cubic meter, which is 44.71% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 117.66 percent of last  year's  storage  and  127.01  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last

year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.

-  Groundwater  Levels:  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
-  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
-  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
-  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests &amp; Diseases : The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level.
