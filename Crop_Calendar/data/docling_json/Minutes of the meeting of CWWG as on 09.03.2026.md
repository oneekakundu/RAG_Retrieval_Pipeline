## Key Insights

1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status: Summer sowing data collection has been started from 9 th  February, 2026 and the sowing is in preliminary stage. As on March 06, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 31.69 lakh ha as compared to 33.38 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 25.30 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
2.  As  on  06.03.2026, 8.64%  of area has been harvested  for Rabi crops all over India. Approximately 20% area of Pulses mainly in Gram (21%) &amp; Urdbean (46%); 15.51% area of Oilseeds  majorly  in  Rapeseed  &amp;  Mustard  (16.57%);  12.04%  area  of  Shri  Anna  cum  Coarse Cereals majorly in Jowar (15.94%) and 16.74% area of Rice has been harvested for rabi crops 2025-26.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water Reservoir Status : As on 05 March 2026, the total live water storage in 166 important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 104.133 billion cubic meter, which is 57%  of  total  live  storage  capacity  (183.565  BCM).  The  current  year's  storage  is  nearly 112.86 percent of last year's storage and 126.84 percent of the average storage of the last ten years during the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
- b) Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  around  90%  of  the  country's  monitoring stations  exhibited  a  depth  to  water  level  within  10  meters  below  ground  level.  Deeper water  levels  of  more  than  20  m  are  observed  in  around  5%  of  stations  of  the  country covering  mainly  the western  states,  especially  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh.
3.  The deeper groundwater level in the states like Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh  during  post-monsoon  2025  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over extraction of groundwater compared to the recharge during the monsoon period.
4.  Annual  water  level  comparison  with  the  previous  year  Post-monsoon  2024  to  Postmonsoon  2025  has  shown  that  about  63%  of  total  analyzed  stations  of  the  country experienced rise in  annual  water level fluctuation may  be  attributed to the less groundwater extraction compared to 2024.
5.  About 73% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level  in  the  decadal  mean  water  level  fluctuation  of  2015-2024  with  respect  to  Postmonsoon 2025.
6.  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in Post-monsoon  2025 ,  groundwater conditions  across  India  remained  largely  stable,  with 91%  of  monitoring  stations showing no  deficit .  However,  localized  deficits  were  observed  in  several  regions  notably in Meghalaya, Nagaland, and the Dadar Nagar Havili &amp; Daman &amp; Diu.

- c) Fertilizers : The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests &amp; Diseases : Intensity of pests and diseases at some fields has been reported to be above the Economic Threshold Level for Citrus (Gummosis) and Chilli (Leaf curl) in Maharashtra. The  Advisory  has  been  issued  by  the  survey  team  to  the  farms  and  regular  monitoring  is being carried out.
