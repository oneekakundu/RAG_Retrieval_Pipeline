## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on May 22, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 86.02 lakh ha as compared to 83.50 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 31.05 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year. There is also an increase in the area sown under Maize by about 1.50 lakh ha so far from the last 5-years average.
-  As on 22.05.2026, 97.92% of area  has  been  harvested  for  Rabi  crops  all  over  India.  The  harvest  of pulses,  oilseeds,  and  Shri  Anna-cum-coarse  cereals  has  been  completed;  79.71%  area  of  Rice  and 99.50% area of Wheat has been harvested for Rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

- a) Temperature:  Maximum  temperature  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  in many  parts  of  Northwest India, Chhattisgarh, East India, Northeast India and northern part of Coastal Andhra Pradesh. Heat wave to severe heat wave conditions likely to prevail over northwest and central India during many days of the week.
- b) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  May  24  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  1% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 5%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 8%; (iii) Central India: 19%; and South Peninsula: (-) 3%.
- c) A fresh Western Disturbance likely to affect Northwest India from 28th May, 2026.
- d) Isolated  heavy  to  very  rainfall  likely  over  Kerala  &amp;  Lakshadweep,  Tamil  Nadu,  Northeast  &amp; adjoining east India during next 4-5 days.
- e) Isolated light to moderate rainfall with thunderstorm, lightning &amp; gusty winds likely over Vidarbha during  26th-28th;  Konkan  &amp;  Goa  on  27th  &amp;  28th;  Madhya  Maharashtra  during  25th-28 th , Marathawada during 25th-27th May; Punjab, Haryana &amp; Chandigarh, West Uttar Pradesh during 28th-30th; East Uttar Pradesh during 25th-29th and Rajasthan on 30th May.
- f) Heat wave to severe heat wave conditions likely to continue to prevail over Central &amp; Northwest India  during  next 7  days;  East &amp;  adjoining Peninsular  India  during  next  3-5  days. Reduction  in maximum temperatures likely from 29th May onwards.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  21  May  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 60.830 billion cubic meter, which is 33.14% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 111.95 percent of last  year's  storage  and  124.39  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
2.  Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
3.  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
4.  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
5.  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests  &amp;  Diseases :  Intensity  of  pests  and  diseases  at  some  fields  has  been  reported  to  be  above Economic Threshold Level for Litchi (Stinkbug and Mealy bug) in Bihar and for Rice (Neck Blast) in Assam. The Advisory has been issued by the survey team to the farms and regular monitoring is being carried out. The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level
