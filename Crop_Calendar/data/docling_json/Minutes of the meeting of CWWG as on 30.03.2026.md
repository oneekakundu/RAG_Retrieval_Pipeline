## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

As on March 27, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 49.87 lakh ha as compared to 52.35 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 28.50 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.

-  As on 27.03.2026, 32.26% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. Approximately 67% area of Pulses mainly in Gram (66%), Urdbean (86%) &amp; Moong (63%); 71.56% area of Oilseeds majorly in  Rapeseed  &amp; Mustard (75.53%); 38.31% area of Shri Anna cum Coarse Cereals majorly in Jowar (54.47%), 25.57% area of Rice and 6.00% area of Wheat  has been harvested for Rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

Temperature: Maximum Temperature is likely to be above normal in many parts of Northwest India, many parts of East India, Chhattisgarh, many parts of Coastal Andhra Pradesh, Coastal regions of Tamil Nadu, North Kerala, Coastal Karnataka and Konkan-Goa.

- a) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  March  30  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  7% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 71%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 34%; (iii) Central India: (-) 7%; and South Peninsula: 26%.
2.  A fresh Western Disturbance is likely to affect Northwest India from 02nd April, 2026.
3.  Wet spell likely to continue over northwest India during the week with peak activity on 29 th &amp; 30 th March and isolated heavy rainfall/snowfall over Kashmir valley on 30th March, 2026.
4.  Isolated Hailstorm s likely over Northwest India during 29-30 March, and over Central part of India during 30-31 st March 2026.
5.  Scattered to fairly widespread rainfall along with thunderstorm/lightning &amp; gusty wind likely over Northeast  &amp;  adjoining  east  India  during  the  week  with  possibility  of  isolated  heavy  falls  over Northeast India during 30 March-01 April, 2026.
6.  Isolated rainfall along with thunderstorm, lightning &amp; gusty winds likely to continue over South Peninsular India during the week.
7.  Widespread  rainfall  activity  likely  over  Northeast  India,  Jammu  &amp;  Kashmir  adjoining  Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Tamil Nadu and Isolated rainfall activity over Uttarakhand &amp; adjoining plains of western Himalayan region, Central India, Odisha &amp; adjoining Coastal Andhra Pradesh.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water Reservoir Status: As on 27 March 2026, the total live water storage in 166 important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 85.432 billion cubic meter, which is 46.54% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 110.50 percent of last year's storage and 121.03 percent of the average storage of the last ten years during the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
- b) Groundwater  Levels:  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  around  90%  of  the  country's  monitoring  stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
3.  The deeper groundwater level in the states like Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh during post-monsoon 2025 has been significantly influenced by the over extraction of groundwater compared to the recharge during the monsoon period.
4.  Annual water level comparison with the previous year Post-monsoon 2024 to Post-monsoon 2025 has shown that about 63% of total analyzed stations of the country experienced rise in annual water level fluctuation may be attributed to the less groundwater extraction compared to 2024.
5.  About 73% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in the decadal mean water level fluctuation of 2015-2024 with respect to Post-monsoon 2025.
6.  The Groundwater Drought Indices indicate that in Post-monsoon 2025 ,  groundwater  conditions across  India  remained  largely  stable,  with 91%  of  monitoring  stations showing no  deficit . However, localized deficits were observed in several regions - notably in Meghalaya, Nagaland, and the Dadra Nagar Havili &amp; Daman &amp; Diu.
- c) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests &amp; Diseases : The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level.
