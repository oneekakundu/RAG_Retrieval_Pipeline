## Key Insights

1. Kharif Sowing Status: As on 05 September, 2025, the area sown under Kharif crops is 1105.42 lakh ha as compared to 1078.49 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year. About 100.8 percent of the normal area has been sown under the Kharif season crops. The status of sowing under Rice, Pulses, Coarse Cereals and Sugarcane is better than in the same period of last year.

- a) Temperature: Maximum temperatures is likely to be above normal in East India, NE India, Chhattisgarh, Coastal Andhra Pradesh and South Karnataka .
- b) Rainfall Distribution: From June 01 -Sept 07 2025, the All-India cumulative rainfall is 9% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India:(-)20%; (ii) Northwest India: 37%; (iii) Central India: 11%; and South Peninsula: 08%.

It  is  very  likely  to  move  nearly  westwards  as  a  deep depression across southeast Pakistan and adjoining Rajasthan till 8 th afternoon. Thereafter,  it  will  continue  to  move  nearly  westwards  and  weaken  gradually  into  a depression.
- Heavy to very heavy rainfall likely over Rajasthan and Gujarat state till 08 th  September with  possibility  of  Extremely  heavy  falls  at  isolated  places  over  Rajasthan  &amp;  south Gujarat, also exceptionally heavy falls ( ≥ 30 cm) over north Gujarat region and Saurashtra &amp; Kutch.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  04 September  2025,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  161 important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 158.39 billion cubic meter, which is 87% of total live storage capacity (182.479 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 106.8 percent of last year's storage and 121.9 percent of the average storage of the last ten years during the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
