## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on May 01, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 81.60 lakh ha as compared to 79.00 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 31.05 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
-  As on 01.05.2026, 95.25% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over India. 100% area of Pulses has been completed; 99.87% area of Oilseeds majorly in Rapeseed &amp; Mustard (100%); 96.22% area of Shri Anna cum Coarse Cereals majorly in Jowar (98.86%), 59.32% area of Rice and 97.00% area of Wheat has been harvested for rabi crops 2025-26.

## 2. Weather Update:

Temperature:  Maximum  temperature  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  in  West Rajasthan,  some  parts  of Saurashtra-Kutch and some parts of Odisha.

- a) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  May  26  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  4% higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 17%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 9%; (iii) Central India: 31%; and South Peninsula: (-) 23%.
2.  Fairly widespread to widespread rainfall along with thunderstorm, lightning and gusty winds with heavy rainfall likely over Sub Himalayan West Bengal &amp; Sikkim and northeast India during 03rd 05th May.
3.  Isolated to scattered thunderstorms, lightning &amp; gusty winds and hailstorm likely over Northwest, central and East India during 03rd-05th May, 2026.
4.  Isolated  to  scattered  thunderstorms,  lightning  &amp;  gusty  winds  and  isolated  heavy  rainfall  likely Tamil Nadu, Puducherry &amp; Karaikal, South Interior Karnataka, Coastal Andhra Pradesh &amp; Yanam and Kerala &amp; Mahe during the week.
5.  Widespread rainfall activity likely over Northeast India and adjoining East India.
6.  Isolated  to  Scattered  rainfall  activity  likely  over  North  India  and  Southern  parts  of  Peninsular India.
7.  Hot  &amp;  humid  weather  conditions  very  likely  to  prevail  in  isolated  pockets  over  Tamil  Nadu, Puducherry &amp; Karaikal and Coastal Andhra Pradesh &amp; Yanam on 04th May and over Konkan &amp; Goa during 5th -7th May.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  30  April  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 71.082 billion cubic meter, which is 38.72% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 114.10 percent of last  year's  storage  and  126.53  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.
2.  Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
3.  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana  and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
4.  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
5.  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
