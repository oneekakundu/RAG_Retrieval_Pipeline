## Key Insights

## 1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:

-  As on April 24, 2026, the area sown under summer crops is 72.30 lakh ha as compared to 70.19 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.  Of this, area of 30.68 lakh ha has been sown under Rice this year.
-  As on 24.04.2026, 92.62% of area has been harvested for Rabi crops all over  India. Approximately 99.58%  area  of  Pulses  mainly  in  Gram  (100%),  Urdbean  (99%)  &amp;  Moong  (86%);  99.50%  area  of Oilseeds  majorly  in  Rapeseed  &amp;  Mustard  (100%);  92.43%  area  of  Shri  Anna  cum  Coarse  Cereals majorly in Jowar (96.97%), 47.89% area of Rice and 94.00% area of Wheat has been harvested for Rabi crops 2025-26.

Temperature:  Maximum  temperature  is  likely  to  be  above  normal  in  Northwest  India, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Odisha, Arunachal Pradesh, Konkan-Goa, Coastal Karnataka, Kerala and Tamil Nadu.

- a) Rainfall  Distribution:  From  March  01-  April  26  2026,  the  All-India  cumulative  rainfall  is  3% lower than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East &amp; Northeast India: 9%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 12%; (iii) Central India: 14%; and South Peninsula: (-) 26%.

5.  Isolated  heavy  rainfall likely  over  Arunachal  Pradesh  on  23 rd &amp;  during  25 th -29 th ;  Assam  &amp; Meghalaya during 25 th -29 th ; Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram &amp; Tripura on 28 th &amp; 29 th ; with isolated very heavy rainfall likely over Arunachal Pradesh during 27 th -29 th ; Assam &amp; Meghalaya during 26 th -29 th April.

## 3. Inputs Situation

- a) Water  Reservoir  Status :  As  on  23  April  2026,  the  total  live  water  storage  in  166  important reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 74.999 billion cubic meter, which is 40.86% of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 114.71 percent of last  year's  storage  and  126.17  percent  of  the  average  storage  of  the  last  ten  years  during  the corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last

year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period.

-  Groundwater  Levels :  In  Post-monsoon  2025,  In  January,  2026,  around  75.8%  of  the  country's monitoring stations exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.
-  The  deeper  groundwater  level  in  the  states  like  Western  Rajasthan,  Punjab,  Haryana and Chandigarh  during  January,  2026  has  been  significantly  influenced  by  the  over  extraction  of groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.
-  About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.
-  The  Groundwater  Drought  Indices  indicate  that  in  January,  2026,  groundwater  conditions  across India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland.
- b) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products.
4. Pests &amp; Diseases : The overall intensity of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold Level.
