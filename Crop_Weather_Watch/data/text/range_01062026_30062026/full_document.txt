Key Insights 
1. Rabi Season harvest and Summer Season Sowing Status:  
 As per 3rd Advance Estimates 2025-26, the area sown under the summer crops is 87.26 lakh ha as 
compared to 83.91 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year. As compared to previous year, 
Rice area declined by 0.65 lakh ha whereas, Maize area increased by 2.08 lakh ha, Groundnut by 1.03 
lakh ha, Urad by 0.76 lakh ha and Shree Anna by 0.70 lakh ha.  
 
 As on 01.06.2026, rabi crops harvesting has been completed in all states. 
 
2. Weather Update:  
a) Temperature: Maximum temperature is likely to be below normal in most of country except in 
Northeast India. 
 
b) Rainfall Distribution: From March 01– May 31 2026, the All-India cumulative rainfall is 1% 
higher than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East & Northeast India: 
1%; (ii) Northwest India: (-) 6%; (iii) Central India: 17%; and South Peninsula: 0%. 
 The Western Disturbance as a cyclonic circulation over northwest Uttar Pradesh & neighbourhood 
between 3.1 & 7.6 km above mean sea level with a trough aloft in middle & upper tropospheric 
westerlies with its axis at 5.8 km above mean sea level roughly along Long 78°E to the north of 
Lat 20°N persists.  
 Isolated heavy rainfall likely over Northeast India and South Peninsular India during the week. 
 Moderate to Severe Thunderstorm activity with squally wind speed upto 90kmph and isolated 
hailstorm likely over Northwest, Central & adjoining East India on 31st May. Isolated Hailstorm 
activity likely over Odisha, West Madhya Pradesh and Madhya Maharashtra on 01st June. 
 Normal to above normal rainfall is expected over central and northwest India. 
 Heat wave condition likely to prevail over Bihar during 04th–06th June.  
 
 
3.  Inputs Situation 
 
a) Water Reservoir Status: As on 29 May 2026, the total live water storage in 166 important 
reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 56.300 billion cubic meter, which is 30.67% 
of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 100.73 percent of 
last year's storage and 119.11 percent of the average storage of the last ten years during the 
corresponding period. The overall storage position is better than the corresponding period of last 
year in the country as a whole and is also better than the Normal storage during the corresponding 
period.   
b) Groundwater Levels: In January, 2026, around 75.8% of the country's monitoring stations 
exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more 
than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western 
states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.

[PAGE 3]
The deeper groundwater level in the states like Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and 
Chandigarh during January, 2026 has been significantly influenced by the over extraction of 
groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.  
About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in 
January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.  
The Groundwater Drought Indices indicate that in January, 2026, groundwater conditions across 
India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in 
respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions — 
notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, and Nagaland. 
 
 
 
c) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products. 
 
 
4. Pests & Diseases: The overall incidence of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold 
Level. The monitoring of Cotton crop for Pink Ball Worm is progressing as per the protocol.
