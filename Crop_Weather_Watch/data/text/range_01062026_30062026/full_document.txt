Key Insights 
1. Kharif Season Sowing Status:  
 Kharif sowing data collection has started from 05th June, 2026 and the sowing is in preliminary 
stage. As on 12 June 2026, the area sown under Kharif crops is 84.60 lakh ha (including 54.08 lakh 
ha in Sugarcane) as compared to 88.04 lakh ha during the corresponding period of last year.   
 
2. Weather Update:  
a) 
Temperature: Maximum temperature is likely to be above normal in Madhya Maharashtra, East 
India, Rajasthan and many parts of Northeast India. 
b) Rainfall Distribution: From June 01– 14 June 2026, the All-India cumulative rainfall is 8% lower 
than its Long Period Average (LPA), with regional variations: (i) East & Northeast India:           
(-) 40%; (ii) Northwest India: 13%; (iii) Central India: (-) 55%; and South Peninsula: (-) 4%. 
 The seasonal trough at mean sea level from West Rajasthan to Gangetic West Bengal across 
northeast Rajasthan, north Madhya Pradesh, south Uttar Pradesh, Bihar and Jharkhand persists. 
 Isolated heavy to very heavy rainfall likely over Sub-Himalayan West Bengal & Sikkim, 
Arunachal Pradesh and Assam & Meghalaya during next 6-7 days.  
 Rainfall activity is likely in South India, some parts of Maharashtra, NE India, some parts of 
Central India, J&K, and isolated places of Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, and 
Uttarakhand. 
 Rainfall is likely to be above normal in Kerala, some parts of South Interior Karnataka, 
Arunachal Pradesh, while it is below normal in coastal Karnataka, Konkan-Goa, Gujarat, 
Odisha and East & Northeast India.  
 Isolated to Scattered rainfall likely over many parts of Northwest India and some parts of 
Central India during 14th - 20th June. 
 Heat wave conditions very likely in isolated pockets over Marathawada and Vidarbha during 
next 3-4 days. 
 
 
3.  Inputs Situation 
 
a) Water Reservoir Status: As on 11 June 2026, the total live water storage in 166 important 
reservoirs across the country (monitored by CWC) is 51.917 billion cubic meter, which is 28.28% 
of total live storage capacity (183.565 BCM). The current year's storage is nearly 91.83 percent of 
last year's storage and 115.80 percent of the average storage of the last ten years during the 
corresponding period. The overall storage position is less than the corresponding period of last year 
in the country as a whole but is better than the Normal storage during the corresponding period. 
b) Groundwater Levels: In January, 2026, around 75.8% of the country's monitoring stations 
exhibited a depth to water level within 10 meters below ground level. Deeper water levels of more 
than 20 m are observed in around 13.5% of stations of the country covering mainly the western 
states, especially Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh.

[PAGE 3]
The deeper groundwater level in the states like Western Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana and 
Chandigarh during January, 2026 has been significantly influenced by the over extraction of 
groundwater compared to the recharge in the area, indicating negative water balance.  
About 70% of the analyzed stations of the unconfined aquifer experienced a rise of water level in 
January, 2026 when compared to the decadal mean water level of 2016-2025.  
The Groundwater Drought Indices indicate that in January, 2026, groundwater conditions across 
India remained largely stable, with 90.30% of monitoring stations falling under normal condition in 
respect of Ground Water Drought. However, localized deficits were observed in several regions — 
notably in Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, and Nagaland. 
 
 
 
c) Fertilizers: The fertilizer supply remains stable across all products. 
 
 
4. Pests & Diseases: The overall incidence of pests and diseases remained below the Economic Threshold 
Level.

RAINFALL 


Rainfall Forecast: 
 Rainfall activity is likely in South India, West India, Vidarbha, some parts of Central India, 
Chhattisgarh, Odisha, NE India, J&K, some parts of Himachal Pradesh, isolated places in 
Uttarakhand. 
 Overall, rainfall is likely to be below normal over the entire country except Konkan & Goa, Madhya 
Maharashtra, Marathwada, Karnataka, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu, where it 
will be near normal to above normal during the week.
